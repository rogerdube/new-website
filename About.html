Roger Dube        
Emeritus Professor, Physics

Dr. Dube received his bachelor's degree in experimental physics from Cornell University and his PhD in experimental physics from Princeton University. Currently Professor Emeritus at RIT and a former Assistant Dean for Undergraduate Research, he has been a visiting Fulbright scholar at the University of Manitoba, he has held positions at Kitt Peak and Caltech’s JPL, the University of Michigan, the University of Arizona and Yale. He rose to the executive level at IBM’s Research Division. He has served the National Academy of Sciences in its grant selection process for its Ford Foundation Graduate Program for Minorities, has been an entrepreneur and has started two companies within the last 18 years. He is active in developing Native American programs at RIT and University of Manitoba. He has mentored students throughout the years as they worked on bachelors, masters and PhD degrees. Dr. Dube helped the University of Manitoba secure a conference grant.... Dr. Dube, an enrolled member of the Abenaki Nation of Missisquoi and also Mohawk Turtle clan, is a Sequoyah Fellow of AISES. He was recently awarded the Ely Parker Award from the American Indian Science and Engineering Society, their highest honor. He holds 21 issued patents and has published a textbook on physics-based computer security. He is cofounder of the Native Alumni of Princeton Affinity Group, and has been an alumni interviewer since 2008.
